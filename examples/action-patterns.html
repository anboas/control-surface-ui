Operational control

Actions should be obvious, bounded, reversible where possible, and stable under pressure

These specimens compose existing buttons, icon buttons, popovers, tabs, chips, alerts, search, fields, menus, and data rows into reusable action surfaces. The differences are selection state, risk level, scope, density, and whether the action is primary, secondary, or destructive.

Pattern 12

Implementation contract

Rules for composing action surfaces across the framework.

  • Put the most common action first and keep destructive actions visually separated.
  • Reserve icon-only buttons for familiar symbols with labels or tooltips.
  • Let text shrink with ellipsis while icons, badges, and chevrons keep fixed geometry.
  • Use batch bars and action sheets when the scope changes from one item to many.
